Static Friction

Static Friction Rules

Whenever a block is sliding on the table, it experiences the force of kinetic friction. Whenever the block is not moving on the table, but there is a force attempting to push it, it experiences the force of static friction.

Static friction is more complicated than kinetic friction. While kinetic friction always has the same value, static friction adjusts to the situation.

Static Friction Formulas

The formula for static friction is more complex than other physics formulas. It does not simply involve plugging in, but requires reasoning based upon the situation. This type of force is called a constraint force. $$ F_{fr} \leq \mu_s \cdot F_N $$ or $$ F_{fr}(MAX) = \mu_s \cdot F_N $$

How the magnitude of static friction is determined (in cases with only one applied force):

  • If the applied force is less than the maximum static friciton, the magnitude of static friction EQUALS the magnitude of the applied force.
  • If the applied force is greater than the maximum value of static friciton, the object begins to move, and the static friction is replaced by kinetic friction. (The magnitude of kinetic friction is always weaker than the maximum static friction).
More Static Friction Facts
  • The direction of static friction is always resisting the force that is attempting to move the object.
  • The coefficient of static friction is always greater than the coefficient of kinetic friction. This is why it takes more force to begin pushing a box than to keep it moving.

Situation 1

A box is stationary on a floor with friction.
The mass of the block is 2.00 kg.
An applied force of 5.00 Newtons is pushing the block to the right.
The coefficient of static friction is 0.800 and the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.400.

  1. Determine the magnitude of gravity and of the normal force.
  2. Determine the maximum magnitude of static friction with the formula \( F_{fr}(MAX) = \mu_S \cdot F_N \).
  3. A block moves if the applied force exceeds the maximum magnitude of static friction. Does this block move?
  4. What type of friction do you have, and what is the magnitude of the force of friction?
    • If the box is moving, then you have kinetic friction and the magnitude of friction is give by \( F_{fr} = \mu_K \cdot F_N \)
    • If the box is not moving, then you ahve static friction. The net force is zero, and thus the friction must equal the magnitude of the applied force.
